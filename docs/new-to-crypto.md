# The Basics, No Jargon

New to all this? Perfect — this page is for you. No math, no scary words. Just
enough to feel comfortable using Olio.

## Blockchain = a public spreadsheet

A blockchain is a giant shared spreadsheet that lives on thousands of computers.
Every payment is a new row. The catch: **anyone can read it.** No login, no
permission. That's great for trust, and terrible for privacy — which is exactly
the gap Olio fills.

Olio runs on **Stellar**, a blockchain built to move money quickly and cheaply.

## USDC = digital dollars

**USDC** is a "stablecoin" — a digital dollar. One USDC is meant to always equal
one US dollar. Unlike coins that swing wildly in price, USDC stays put, which
makes it great for actually getting paid. When someone pays you through Olio,
they pay in USDC.

## Wallets & addresses

A **wallet** is your account. It has an **address** — a long string of letters and
numbers — which is like an account number people can send money to.

The problem: that address is public and permanent. Share it once and it's
forever linked to you. Olio hides this behind a friendly **username** instead, and
keeps your incoming payments private.

## "Gas" fees (that you won't pay)

Most blockchains make you pay a tiny network fee — called **gas** — for every
action, using a special token. It's annoying for newcomers.

Good news: **Olio covers these fees for you.** You don't need to buy or hold any
gas token just to receive money or cash out.

## On-chain privacy risks (the short version)

Because the spreadsheet is public, using a normal wallet means:

* people can see your balance and income,
* people can see who paid you,
* and once they know your address, they can watch it forever.

Olio's job is to make sure that even though the blockchain is public, **your
payments don't reveal any of that.**

## Getting paid with Olio

1. You claim a username.
2. You share your link or QR code.
3. Your client pays in USDC.
4. The money shows up as a **private balance** only you can see.

## Cashing out to real money

When you want actual dollars in your bank, Olio connects to a regulated service
called an **anchor** that handles the crypto-to-bank conversion. You enter your
bank details with them directly — Olio never sees them. More in
[Cashing Out](cashing-out.md).
